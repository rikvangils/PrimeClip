# Domain Research Workflow
## ProjectFire: AI Content Creation & Virtual Creators Industry

**Agent:** Business Analyst (bmm-analyst)  
**Module:** Business Modeling (BMM)  
**Output:** `docs/domain-research.md`  
**Duration:** 60-90 minutes (deep technical + industry research)

---

## Workflow Objective

Conduct comprehensive domain research on:

1. **AI Content Generation Technology** - Which tools, capabilities, evolution
2. **Virtual Creator Industry** - Who's building what, success patterns, failures
3. **Regulatory & Policy Landscape** - What rules govern AI content
4. **Technical Architecture Patterns** - How do successful AI creator operations work
5. **Content Distribution Strategy** - How to reach audiences with AI content
6. **Tool Ecosystem & Accessibility** - What's available, costs, limitations

---

## Research Sections

### Section 1: Generative AI Tools Landscape

**Research Focus:** What tools exist for image generation? Costs? Quality? Limitations?

**Tools to Research:**
- Midjourney (pricing, capabilities, limitations)
- Stable Diffusion (open-source, local installation, quality)
- DALL-E 3 (OpenAI, capabilities)
- Adobe Firefly (integration potential)
- Runway ML (video + image generation)

**Key Questions:**
- What's the cost per image at scale?
- How much creative control? (prompting, style consistency)
- Can you enforce consistent character design?
- Quality comparison (which produces best anime content?)
- API availability for automation?

### Section 2: AI Avatar & Virtual Creator Platforms

**Research Focus:** What platforms specifically build AI creators/avatars?

**Platforms to Research:**
- HeyGen (AI video avatars)
- Synthesia (AI presenters/videos)
- D-ID (digital avatars)
- Gencraft (AI art generation)
- Artbreeder (character design tools)
- Character.AI (conversational AI)

**Key Questions:**
- How do these generate consistency across content?
- What's the cost structure (one-time, API-based)?
- Can characters be used for social media?
- Licensing & IP ownership?
- Integration with social platforms?

### Section 3: Successful AI Creator Case Studies

**Research Focus:** How did existing AI creators actually build their audiences?

**Case Studies to Research:**
- Lil Miquela (Brud company, Instagram strategy)
- Imma (visual style, content strategy, brand partnerships)
- Project Kaleidoscope (multi-creator platform)
- Noonoouri (fashion/lifestyle positioning)

**Key Questions:**
- How did they grow followers initially?
- What platforms worked best?
- How did they get first brand deals?
- What was their content strategy?
- What failed or was tried but didn't work?
- How much team/investment required?

### Section 4: Content Distribution & Algorithm Strategy

**Research Focus:** How do social media algorithms treat AI-generated content?

**Platforms to Analyze:**
- TikTok algorithm (what it rewards, AI content bias?)
- Instagram algorithm (stills vs. reels, AI detection?)
- YouTube algorithm (quality metrics, consistency rewards)
- Pinterest algorithm (visual search, discovery)

**Key Questions:**
- Do algorithms favor or suppress AI content?
- What content signals matter most? (consistency, engagement, niche)
- How important is "newness" vs. consistency?
- Multi-platform strategy viability?
- Early growth strategies (hashtags, communities, engagement)?

### Section 5: Regulatory & Policy Landscape

**Research Focus:** What laws/policies govern AI-generated content?

**Areas to Research:**
- EU AI Act (classification, transparency requirements)
- Platform policies (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube on AI content)
- Copyright & licensing (can you generate images commercially?)
- Disclosure requirements (must you label AI content?)
- Regional differences (US, EU, Asia)

**Key Questions:**
- Must AI content be labeled/disclosed?
- Are there restrictions on commercial use?
- Liability if user-generated derivative content?
- Creator rights (can you copyright AI generated images?)
- Patent/licensing issues with tools?

### Section 6: Building at Scale - Operational Patterns

**Research Focus:** How do successful creators handle operations at scale?

**Patterns to Research:**
- Content production automation (scheduling, batch creation)
- Community management (responding to engagement)
- Analytics & optimization (what to measure?)
- Revenue operations (managing multiple monetization streams)
- Team scaling (when to hire, what roles)

**Key Questions:**
- How much human time required to scale?
- When does automation break down?
- What tools integrate social platforms?
- How to maintain quality at high volume?
- What metrics matter most (followers vs. engagement vs. revenue)?
