# Product Brief Creation Workflow
## ProjectFire: AI Virtual Creators Network

**Agent:** Product Manager (bmm-pm)
**Module:** Business Modeling (BMM)
**Output:** `docs/product-brief.md`
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes (interactive)

---

## Workflow Process

This is a **collaborative discovery workflow**. The agent will guide you through a series of questions to deeply understand and articulate your product vision.

### Phase 1: Problem & Opportunity (10 min)
**Goal:** Understand what problem you're solving

Questions:
- What is the core problem you're addressing?
- Who has this problem? (audiences/segments)
- Why now? What's changed in the market?
- What are current solutions people are using?
- Why are those solutions inadequate?

**Output:** Problem statement + opportunity articulation

---

### Phase 2: Product Vision & Direction (10 min)
**Goal:** Define what success looks like

Questions:
- In one sentence, what is your product?
- What is the core value proposition?
- What does "done" or "success" look like?
- What is NOT in scope? (what won't you do)
- What is your unfair advantage/moat?

**Output:** Vision statement + strategic positioning

---

### Phase 3: Target Market & Users (8 min)
**Goal:** Define who you're building for

Questions:
- Who is your primary user/audience?
- Which niches will you target first?
- What are their pain points?
- How will they use your product?
- Who else will benefit? (secondary audiences)

**Output:** User personas + niche segmentation

---

### Phase 4: Business Model & Revenue (8 min)
**Goal:** Define how you'll make money

Questions:
- How will you make revenue? (which channels)
- What is your unit economics? (cost per piece/post)
- What are your revenue targets?
- Who pays? (brands, users, platforms)
- What partnerships will you need?

**Output:** Revenue model + financial assumptions

---

### Phase 5: Execution Plan (8 min)
**Goal:** Define the 6-month roadmap

Questions:
- What are your go-to-market phases?
- What do you need to launch MVP?
- What are critical milestones (3, 6, 12 months)?
- What dependencies do you have?
- What is your resource plan? (team, budget, tools)

**Output:** High-level roadmap + milestone definition

---

### Phase 6: Risks & Assumptions (5 min)
**Goal:** Identify what could go wrong

Questions:
- What are your biggest risks? (market, tech, regulatory)
- What assumptions are you making?
- How will you validate assumptions?
- What is your contingency plan?

**Output:** Risk register + validation strategy

---

## Agent Instructions

1. **Start with discovery mindset** - Ask clarifying questions, don't assume
2. **Build on user's initial concept** - We have good foundation from earlier analysis
3. **Challenge assumptions gently** - "What if X happened..." type questions
4. **Stay focused on ProjectFire specifics:**
   - AI transparency is core
   - Multi-platform (Instagram, TikTok, etc.)
   - B2B brand partnerships primary revenue
   - Scalable content generation
   - IP ownership critical
5. **Document decisions** - Capture the reasoning, not just the answers
6. **End with clarity** - Get agreement on direction before moving forward

---

## Success Criteria

✅ Problem clearly articulated
✅ Vision statement clear and compelling
✅ Target niches identified (3 potential)
✅ Revenue model realistic and quantified
✅ 6-month roadmap with clear milestones
✅ Key risks identified and mitigation planned
✅ Buy-in from you (the founder) on this direction

---

## What Happens Next

After Product Brief is approved:

1. **Market Research** - Analyst validates market opportunity
2. **Domain Research** - Analyst deep-dives AI creator industry
3. **Full PRD** - Product Manager creates detailed requirements
4. **UX Design** - Architect plans content flow & user experience
5. **Architecture** - Architect designs technical systems
6. **Epics & Stories** - Analyst breaks into implementation units
7. **Sprint Planning** - Product Manager creates execution plan
